01 — Overview

The Design Problem

Citizens in India’s state governments had no reliable mechanism to file, track, or escalate grievances against government officials. Existing systems were paper-based, officer-dependent, and designed around administrative convenience rather than citizen need. There was no acknowledgement of receipt, no tracking, no automatic escalation when deadlines were missed, and no public record of performance. My role was to co-design a user-centred service from the ground up: mapping user journeys across six filing touchpoints, producing a service blueprint that made the full system legible, specifying interaction design for officer-facing digital forms, and building a performance measurement framework to make district accountability visible.

The Citizen Journey

No way to know if a complaint was received, who was responsible, or what the deadline was. No mechanism to escalate if deadlines were missed without physically visiting an office.

The Officer Experience

No structured format for recording how a complaint was investigated or what action was taken. Officers could close complaints without evidence of resolution.

The System Problem

No public record of performance. No pressure on districts or departments to improve. Senior officials had no reliable data on where the system was failing.
